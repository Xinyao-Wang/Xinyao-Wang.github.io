About

I am a PhD candidate in Mechanical Engineering at UC Riverside. My research focuses on dynamic modeling and control of antagonistic soft actuators, with an emphasis on equilibrium point and impedance modulation for wearable and human-interactive robots.

Selected Research Projects

Physics-Structured Neural ODE Modeling for Antagonistic PAM Joints (click to expand)

Developed and experimentally validated a physics-structured Neural ODE model for antagonistic pneumatic artificial muscle (PAM) joints, enabling direct equilibrium-point and impedance modulation.

  • Embedded mechanical and pneumatic structure while learning unmodeled, rate-dependent dynamics from hardware data.
  • Deployed the learned inverse model via lookup tables for real-time closed-loop control on physical hardware.
  • Reduced joint motion phase lag from −11.6° to −5.8° and reduced velocity-dependent stiffness variation from 3.24% to 0.60% for ≤1 Hz motion, relative to a physics-only baseline.
  • Demonstrated model generalization by training on 29 conditions selected from a 225-condition experimental dataset.
Physics-Based Generative Modeling for Antagonistic PAM Joints (click to expand)

Developed a generative modeling framework for antagonistic PAM joints based on experimentally characterized single-actuator properties.

  • Derived joint equilibrium position and effective stiffness using equilibrium theory.
  • Formulated inverse mappings from desired equilibrium point and impedance to actuator inputs.
  • Identified mass, damping, and stiffness parameters directly from hardware data using optimization-based system identification.
Spinal-Like Stretch Reflex for Impedance Modulation (click to expand)

Explored a biologically inspired stretch reflex mechanism to augment impedance modulation in antagonistic pneumatic artificial muscle joints.

  • Implemented a velocity-dependent reflex feedback law layered on top of a physics-based joint model.
  • Visual comparison of stiffness surfaces indicates up to an approximately 60% increase in achievable joint stiffness at matched equilibrium points.
  • Evaluated behavior through controlled experiments presented at the MEGSA Symposium (2024).
Event-Switched Dynamic Modeling of Actuator Hysteresis (click to expand)

Modeled hysteresis in pneumatic artificial muscles using a switched dynamic system with event-dependent parameters.

  • Represented loading and unloading behavior using shared system states with motion-direction-dependent parameter switching.
  • Identified event-dependent force and dynamic parameters and validated behavior under perturbation experiments on hardware.
  • Captured hysteresis behavior as an emergent property of event-switched dynamics rather than imposing static hysteresis curves.
  • Analyzed identifiability and scalability limits affecting practical system identification.
SMA-Actuated MRI-Compatible Wearable Exoskeleton (click to expand)

Designed and evaluated an MRI-compatible, SMA spring–driven antagonistic finger exoskeleton for rehabilitation applications.

  • Built and tested hardware prototypes with six actuators per finger (three degrees of freedom).
  • Identified tendon friction and limited joint moment arms as dominant causes of dead zones and insufficient torque at high flexion.
  • Integrated EM-based motion sensing to estimate joint angles where rigid encoders were impractical.
