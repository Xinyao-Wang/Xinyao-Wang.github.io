About

I am a PhD candidate in Mechanical Engineering at UC Riverside. My research focuses on dynamic modeling and control of antagonistic soft actuators, with an emphasis on equilibrium point and impedance modulation for wearable and human-interactive robots.

Selected Research Projects

Physics-Based Generative Modeling of Antagonistic Soft Actuators (click to expand)

Developed a physics-based generative modeling framework for antagonistic pneumatic artificial muscle (PAM) joints using experimentally characterized single-actuator force and pressure relationships.

  • Derived joint equilibrium positions and effective stiffness using equilibrium theory.
  • Formulated inverse mappings from desired equilibrium point and impedance to actuator inputs.
  • Evaluated performance on physical systems and analyzed limitations due to actuator mismatch and linear assumptions.
Event-Switched Dynamic Modeling of Actuator Hysteresis (click to expand)

Investigated hysteresis effects in pneumatic artificial muscles using dynamic models with motion-direction-dependent parameter switching.

  • Represented loading and unloading behavior through shared system states with switched parameters.
  • Demonstrated that event-based switching enables hysteresis to emerge in dynamic responses.
  • Identified scalability and identifiability challenges limiting practical system identification.
Hybrid Neural ODE Modeling of Antagonistic Actuator Dynamics (click to expand)

Developed a physics-structured neural ordinary differential equation (Neural ODE) model to capture nonlinear and rate-dependent dynamics of antagonistic soft actuators.

  • objectded known mechanical and pneumatic structure while learning unmodeled effects.
  • Derived an inverse formulation enabling equilibrium point and impedance modulation.
  • Validated experimentally and compared performance against a physics-based model.
