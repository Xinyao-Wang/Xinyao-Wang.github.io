About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Mechanical Engineering at UC Riverside specializing in hardware-validated modeling and control of soft robotic systems. My work focuses on physics-structured Neural ODEs, impedance and equilibrium-point control, and real-time embedded implementation on custom experimental platforms. I design and build complete mechatronic systems including actuators, sensors, embedded firmware, and custom PCBs, and validate algorithms directly on hardware.

Selected Research Projects

Hybrid Neural ODE Control for Antagonistic PAM Joints
  • Designed a physics-structured Neural ODE to model antagonistic PAM joint dynamics with embedded mechanical and pneumatic structure.
  • Built a complete hardware platform integrating PAMs, pressure sensors, encoders, load cells, and BeagleBone real-time control.
  • Developed a JAX training pipeline using adjoint gradients and Tsitouras ODE solver.
  • Collected large-scale datasets with automated 1 kHz acquisition.
  • Reduced phase lag by 49.5% (−11.6° → −5.8%) and stiffness variation from 3.24% → 0.60%.
  • Achieved R² up to 0.92 across the operating space.
  • Demonstrated generalization using 29 / 225 training conditions.
  • Deployed inverse optimization and LUT-based control on hardware.
Physics-Based Generative Modeling for Antagonistic PAM Joints
  • Characterized single-muscle force–length–pressure behavior using automated experiments.
  • Executed systematic pressure sweeps from 0–487 kPa.
  • Built forward generative models mapping pressure to equilibrium position and stiffness.
  • Developed inverse models to compute pressure from desired impedance targets.
  • Identified mass, damping, and stiffness using optimization-based system identification.
  • Achieved ~3.6% average modeling error.
Spinal-Inspired Stretch Reflex Controller
  • Designed and implemented a bio-inspired stretch reflex controller on embedded hardware.
  • Developed event-triggered valve logic for real-time operation.
  • Layered velocity-dependent reflex feedback on top of physics-based joint model.
  • Increased joint stiffness by up to ~60% at matched equilibrium points.
  • Validated using 3D stiffness and joint-angle surfaces under perturbations.
Event-Switched Dynamic Modeling of Actuator Hysteresis
  • Developed an event-switched dynamic model to capture PAM hysteresis.
  • Used direction-dependent parameter switching for loading and unloading phases.
  • Identified regime-specific parameters from hardware experiments.
  • Validated hysteresis as an emergent dynamic behavior.
  • Analyzed identifiability and scalability limits.
SMA-Actuated MRI-Compatible Wearable Exoskeleton
  • Designed an MRI-compatible antagonistic finger exoskeleton driven by SMA springs.
  • Built and tested hardware prototypes with six actuators per finger (3 DOF).
  • Achieved joint ranges: MCP 0–90°, PIP 0–90°, DIP 0–45°.
  • Identified tendon friction and limited joint moment arms as dominant causes of dead zones and torque loss at high flexion.
  • Integrated EM-based motion sensing for joint angle estimation where rigid encoders were impractical.
Custom Pressure Control PCB for PAM Systems

Repository: github.com/Xinyao-Wang/pcb-pressure-controller

  • Designed a custom PCB supporting 16 solenoid valves for multi-channel PAM actuation.
  • Implemented 8-channel I2C pressure sensing with multiplexer to resolve address conflicts.
  • Designed transistor-based valve interface circuitry with diagnostic LEDs.
  • Completed schematic, layout, Gerbers, and BOM in EAGLE.
  • Validated board in closed-loop PAM control experiments.
